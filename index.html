How it works

Each guard has an Attack (⚔) and Defense (🛡). When you place a card, every adjacent (up/down/left/right) enemy card whose defense < your attack flips to your side. If defense = attack, the game flips a coin — a popup will ask you to enter the actual outcome.

Special Directions ✦ — set any of eight arrows on a card via the 3×3 compass in Quick Add or the ✏ editor. A Special Direction wins the battle on that side regardless of who's attacking:
• Attacker's special toward defender → attacker auto-wins the battle.
• Defender's special pointing back at attacker (opposite arrow) → defender auto-wins, no flip.
• Both at once (attacker's special + defender's counter-special) → clash, coin toss.
• Diagonals (↖ ↗ ↙ ↘) only create a battle when the attacker has that diagonal special; orthogonal neighbors always battle (regular Atk/Def, modified by either side's special as above).

Who goes first — the game decides this for you; just click You first or Opponent first to match.

Win condition — most cards on the board after 11 are played wins. The status bar will show the result.

Suggest Best Move evaluates every (card, square) combination for the side whose turn it is. The score is flips you'd take now minus the biggest counter-flip your opponent could land next turn. The recommended card and square get a yellow glow.

Depth & the CLI — the in-browser engine searches 7 plies, which on the worst case (fresh-start, 0 placed) finishes in ~4s. For deeper analysis — including full-game-tree (depth 11) solves from any position — click 📋 Copy for CLI to copy a ready-to-paste shell command and run it in a terminal sitting in solver/ (build once with cd solver && go build -o tarock-solve .). The native Go solver is much faster and prints the same recommendation in 1–40 seconds depending on position.

Placing a card — on touch, tap a hand card (it gets a green ring) then tap an empty square. On desktop you can also drag the card onto a square. Tap an empty cell while a recommendation is showing to confirm the suggested move.

Fixing mistakes — tap a placed card to remove it back to the hand. Long-press (touch) or right-click (desktop) a placed card to flip its owner — handy for correcting a mis-recorded coin-flip outcome without undoing the whole turn.

Blocked square (required) — every Tarock 2.0 match has exactly one stone tile, leaving 11 playable squares. Click 🪨 Block Square, then click the stone tile on the grid. The app won't let you place a card until that's done.

Coin tosses — for every tied battle (equal Atk/Def, or a Special-Direction clash), a popup asks "What did the game say?". The popup labels each prompt with the defender's row N · col M (and pulses the on-board cell) so you know which battle it's asking about when several queue up. The app never randomizes the outcome itself — it records exactly what you enter.

Best move = pessimistic minimax search. The engine recursively considers every (card × empty-square) move, then assumes the opponent picks their best reply, and so on. At every coin-toss branch in the search, it picks the outcome that is worst for you — i.e., it never bets on a flip going your way. The "worst-case lead" number is the score difference you're guaranteed assuming every coin flip goes against you; the suggested move is the safest choice under that assumption.
